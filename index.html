Synopsis

Bagaskara (20), a Jakarta writer who migrated to the city of Jogja. Not with skills or maturity in mind, but a deep question about happiness. The profession he lives in the overseas city actually triggers confusion about the meaning of happiness itself. Until when fate brought Bagas together with a mysterious cassette that suddenly appeared in many of Bagaskara's belongings as a child. Fate did not remain silent, Bagaskara was reunited with Kaluna, the younger sister of Kala, the real figure behind the cassette that disappeared without a trace. Together with Kaluna, Bagaskara dives into every trace in the cassette, revealing Kala's dark truth and the answer to happiness.

Director's Biography and Filmography

Theodorus Bonarsampang Sinaga Director & Screenwriter Yogyakarta, Indonesia — Born 2007

Theodorus Bonarsampang Sinaga is a Yogyakarta-based director and scriptwriter. His work explores themes of death, deferred dreams, and human inner struggles, with a symbolic and poetic visual approach. He began filmmaking in junior high school but became more active in high school with several short films, one of which was Gentari (2024), which won third place at the Gonzaga College festival. In his creative process, Theodorus always incorporates symbolism through visuals, props, and scripts.

Director's Statement

I made this film out of my own restlessness. It’s a feeling that comes when we keep chasing what we call happiness, but life shows us a reality that doesn’t always go the way we want. Honestly, I’ve felt that a lot: the more I reject the situation, the farther happiness seems to get. That feeling became the starting point of this story. Every character in this film also carries a piece of that restlessness. They’re all searching for happiness in their own ways, even when it hurts. Through their journeys, I wanted to see how ideals and reality can collide, and how we react when things don’t turn out the way we hoped.

I chose to tell this story through symbolic visuals, monologues full of metaphors, and a rhythm that feels a bit unique. Because to me, the restlessness of chasing happiness is abstract, messy, and hard to explain. In this film, the story is also conveyed through a poetic verse, spoken as a kind of internal reflection. I chose actors who also have a background in theater, because I felt they could bring more depth and intensity to the characters. Visually, I used distinct color tones to reflect the characters’ different emotional states. The artistic design of the room represents the inner turmoil of the character’s. I hope that whoever watches this can find their own meaning in it, maybe even see a little of themselves in these characters. And even if not, I just hope this film stays in the audience’s thoughts and feelings, even after the screen goes dark.
